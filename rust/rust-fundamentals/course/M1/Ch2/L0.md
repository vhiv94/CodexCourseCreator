# Prechapter check on variables, functions, and control flow

**Cited contract:** [`course/spine.yaml`](../../spine.yaml) (M1 -> Ch2 -> L0), [`course/overview.md`](../../overview.md) (Log Summary CLI arc). **Builds on:** [Ch1/L5 - Checkpoint on first Cargo and CLI input steps](../Ch1/L5.md).

This is a chapter-entry assessment, not a new implementation push. Chapter 1 established the crate, output, and one-line stdin flow; Chapter 2 is where the program starts taking clearer shape through named bindings, helper functions, branches, and repetition. Before we commit to that pacing, pause long enough to surface what already feels familiar from other languages and what still feels hazy in Rust specifically.

The goal is personalization, not gatekeeping. If `let` and conditionals already feel ordinary but Rust function signatures or `match` feel new, that is exactly the kind of signal this check should capture. Short answers are enough, and uncertainty is useful information.

## Assigned task(s)

- **Explain/Review:** Answer a short prechapter assessment in your own words before starting Chapter 2 implementation lessons.
- **Expected outcomes / acceptance criteria:**
  - [ ] You identify which upcoming Chapter 2 ideas already feel familiar.
  - [ ] You identify which parts of mutability, helper functions, or control flow still feel uncertain.
  - [ ] You point out where extra examples, comparisons, or slower pacing would help.
- **Evidence:** A short-answer note or chat response is enough.

Suggested prompt set:

1. Which upcoming Chapter 2 topics feel most familiar already: named variables, mutability, helper functions, `if`, `match`, or loops?
2. Which of those feels least familiar or most likely to be "Rust-shaped" rather than just general programming knowledge?
3. In your own words, what do you expect `let` and `let mut` to mean before reading the lesson?
4. How do helper functions usually fit into your mental model of a CLI program?
5. What kinds of branching or looping patterns have felt natural or awkward in other languages you have used?
6. Where would you want extra examples or a slower explanation in this chapter?
